### Time
- 2h 30 min on first day
- 3h 30min

### Ingredients
- 560g bread flour or Italian 00 flour, with 12% protein content
	- Optional 10% rye
- 50g Sourdough discard
- 480g lukewarm water (27°C to 37°C)
	- Adjust water depending on flour is, you might need to add more water to get the wet dough consistency. So if your dough is dry, don’t be afraid to add more water.
- 15g salt
- 15g honey
- 1 tbsp (~15g) extra virgin olive oil, plus lots more for greasing the tray and drizzling
- 7g dried yeast

# Instructions

### Autolyse
- **Mix:** Flour, 90% water (420g, only add more if needed), yeast, sourdough discard, and honey.
- Combine until no dry flour remains.
- **Rest:** Covered for 30 minutes at room temp.
### Final mix
- **Add:** 15g salt and 1 tbsp olive oil.
- Incorporate by squeezing the dough through your fingers or using a folding motion until the oil is fully absorbed.
- Scrape down the sides
- Stretch and fold from all 4 sides
### Rest + Fold
**3 Stretch and folds** 
1. Stretch and fold from all 4 sides, cover and rest for 30min
2. Stretch and fold from all 4 sides, cover and rest for 30min
3. Stretch and fold from all 4 sides
### Rest over night
 Cover dough and place into **fridge over night**
 - If you want to skip, let it sit for another 40 min at room temperature instead

## Next day

### Transfer to pan
1.  In a 23x33 cm (9x13") tray 
	 - add baking paper 
	 - on top 3 to 4 tbsp of olive oil (be generous with the oil) and spread
 2. Carefully perform one last series of stretches and folds in the bowl and gently place the dough into your tray. Drizzle with more olive oil.
 3. Gently stretch to the corners.
### Proof
 1. Cover well and proof for _around 2 hours_ at room temperature (still cold from the fridge)
	 - If you skipped cold retard, let proof for ~1 hour 

### Sauce (optional)
- **200g** Crushed tomatoes or thick passata
- **3g** Fine sea salt
- **1 tsp** Dried oregano
- **1 tbsp** Extra virgin olive oil
- **1 small clove** Garlic, finely minced or grated

- Stir all ingredients in a bowl until the salt is dissolved.
- Let rest at room temp. This allows the garlic and oregano to infuse the tomatoes.
### Dimple and Bake
- Preheat the oven to 220°C (Ober- und Unterhitze)
- Drizzle the dough with more olive oil (yes, really!).
	- Cover your hands with oil, and gently make dimples in the dough. Jiggle the dough to spread the it out in the pan.
   - Add toppings of your choice (no sauces yet)
	   - E.g. cherry tomatoes cut in half and olives
	   - OR top with coarse salt, olives and rosemary
   - **Tomate Sauces** *(optional)*
	   - Bake for 10 minutes (crust should be hardened)
	   - Remove from oven and add a **thin** layer of tomatoe sauce
	   - Put back into oven and back for another 15-25 minutes (edges are golden and crispy)
   - **No Sauce**
	   - Bake 25 to 35 minutes (it's done when the top is golden and crispy).
   - Once it gets dark, cover with aluminium foil and bake another 10 minutes until inner temp is 99°C
   - Remove from oven and brush with olive oil and add a bit of salt
   - Let it rest for at least 10 - 15 min

Add the toppings of you choice, making sure the toppings are oiled to prevent burning. And bake.

## Bake the focaccia

- Preheat the oven to 220°C (Ober- und Unterhitze)
- bake 30 to 35 minutes (it's done when the top is golden and crispy).
	- Cover top with aluminium foil and bake for another 10 min
	- Inner temperature should be 99°C
	- If bottom does not bake through, cover top with aluminium foil
- Remove from oven and wait for at least 10 to 15 minutes before slicing into it.

### Reheat the next day
Put it in the oven at 175°C for about 10 minutes to re-crisp the outside and warm the interior.
